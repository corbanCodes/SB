Juneau School District

ADA access for Indigenous families through collaboration over profit

The Priority

Juneau School District placed a high priority on ADA compliance, especially for resources intended for Native and Indigenous families. A key challenge was the lack of readily available translation tools for specific Native languages in the community.

Partnership Approach

SchoolBlocks worked side-by-side with district staff and community advisors, prioritizing student outcomes over profit. Together, we focused on inclusive access—clear layouts, readable contrast, screen-reader compatibility, and content structure that respects community needs—even without direct language software.

Key Outcomes

  • Accessible templates adopted across departments
  • Improved readability and navigation for families
  • Content review workflow with community input
  • Increased trust and participation from stakeholders

Implementation

Co-design sessions, phased accessibility audits, and training for staff on headings, alt text, and document remediation. Adjustments to color contrast and page patterns helped ensure consistent ADA-friendly experiences.

Community Impact

The effort highlighted a shared commitment to inclusion and set a practical foundation for future language support as technology evolves.

Larkspur Unified School District

Selecting a partner over a conveyor-belt implementation

Evaluation Context

Larkspur conducted an exhaustive review of K-12 website vendors. The district prioritized stability, collaboration, and responsiveness over aggressive, VC-driven growth models and one-size-fits-all processes.

Why SchoolBlocks

SchoolBlocks demonstrated a partnership mindset—agile, attentive, and focused on district needs. Rather than a conveyor belt approach, the team tailored implementation and support to Larkspur’s context and goals.

Implementation Approach

Phased rollout with district stakeholders, clear ownership of content migration, and practical training. Processes were documented to avoid lock-in and support internal capacity.

Early Results

  • Smoother launch with fewer support tickets
  • Faster page updates by site managers
  • Clearer governance around content ownership
  • Positive staff feedback on collaboration

Partnership Next Steps

Ongoing check-ins and iterative improvements ensure the platform evolves with district needs rather than locking into a rigid model.

Aspen School District

Simple, mobile-ready admin for timely updates during weather events

The Situation

After a significant additional spend on training failed to equip staff to update the website quickly during weather emergencies, Aspen sought a platform that was simpler, faster, and less time-consuming to use.

Why SchoolBlocks

SchoolBlocks’ streamlined administrator experience works the same on mobile and desktop, allowing authorized staff to publish alerts and updates directly from their phones with minimal steps.

Early Outcomes

  • Faster posting of closures and delays
  • Reduced reliance on specialized web staff
  • More staff confident publishing from mobile
  • Clearer, more consistent alert formatting

Operational Simplicity

Streamlined workflows, fewer steps to publish, and consistent interfaces across devices reduced training requirements and made urgent updates routine rather than stressful.

Community Response

Families reported greater confidence in receiving timely, accurate information—especially during rapidly changing weather conditions.

Oakwood Academy

Mobile-First Strategy Drives Enrollment Success

Mobile-First Vision

Oakwood Academy, a private K-12 institution, recognized that prospective families primarily researched schools on mobile devices. Their desktop-focused website was losing potential enrollments to competitors with better mobile experiences, prompting a complete mobile-first redesign strategy.

Responsive Excellence

SchoolBlocks created a mobile-first design that prioritized fast loading, intuitive navigation, and compelling visual storytelling. The new site featured optimized images, streamlined forms, and touch-friendly interfaces that showcased the academy's unique value proposition.

Mobile Metrics

  • 95% mobile traffic within 6 months
  • 450% increase in enrollment inquiries
  • 3.2 second average load time
  • 85% mobile conversion rate

Enrollment Impact

The mobile-optimized experience directly contributed to a 35% increase in enrollment applications. Families reported that the smooth mobile experience gave them confidence in the school's commitment to modern education approaches.

Industry Recognition

Oakwood Academy's mobile strategy earned recognition from the Private School Marketing Association and has been featured in educational technology publications as a model for mobile-first school marketing.

Northside Independent School District

Crisis Communication Excellence in Action

Communication Challenge

Northside ISD, serving 28,000 students across 45 schools, faced critical communication challenges during emergency situations. Their fragmented notification system took hours to reach all families, creating anxiety and confusion during weather emergencies and security incidents.

Integrated Solution

SchoolBlocks implemented a comprehensive crisis communication system featuring multi-channel notifications, automated translation services, and real-time status updates. The system integrates with local emergency services and provides instant district-wide communication capabilities.

Speed Results

  • 15 minutes to reach 100% of families
  • 99.8% delivery success rate
  • 8 languages simultaneous translation
  • 24/7 availability guaranteed

Crisis Performance

During recent severe weather events, the system successfully notified all 28,000 families within 12 minutes, including automated school closure decisions and transportation updates. Parent anxiety decreased significantly with timely, accurate information.

Emergency Preparedness

Northside ISD now serves as a model for emergency communication best practices. The district has shared their protocols with state emergency management agencies and other school districts seeking to improve crisis communication capabilities.

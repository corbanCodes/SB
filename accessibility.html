SchoolBlocks is dedicated to providing an inclusive educational technology platform that serves all members of the school community. We believe that accessibility is not just a legal requirement, but a fundamental aspect of creating equitable educational experiences.

Our Goal: To ensure that all users, including those with disabilities, can effectively access, navigate, and interact with our platform to achieve their educational objectives.

1. Our Commitment to Accessibility

SchoolBlocks is committed to making our platform accessible to people with disabilities. We continuously work to improve the user experience for everyone and apply relevant accessibility standards.

We strive to comply with applicable accessibility laws and regulations, including:

  • Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) - Ensuring equal access to public accommodations
  • Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act - Prohibiting disability discrimination in federally funded programs
  • Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act - Federal accessibility standards for electronic content
  • State Accessibility Laws - Various state-level requirements for educational institutions

Ongoing Efforts

Our accessibility efforts include:

  • Regular accessibility audits and testing
  • User feedback integration and response
  • Staff training on accessibility best practices
  • Collaboration with accessibility experts and organizations
  • Continuous improvement of our platform's accessibility features

2. Standards and Guidelines

We follow internationally recognized accessibility standards to ensure our platform meets the needs of users with diverse abilities.

Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1

Our platform aims to conform to WCAG 2.1 Level AA standards. These guidelines are organized around four main principles:

Perceivable Level AA

Information and user interface components must be presentable to users in ways they can perceive.

  • Text alternatives for images and media
  • Captions and transcripts for videos
  • Sufficient color contrast ratios
  • Resizable text up to 200% without loss of functionality

Operable Level AA

User interface components and navigation must be operable by all users.

  • Full keyboard accessibility
  • No content that causes seizures or physical reactions
  • Sufficient time limits for timed content
  • Clear navigation and page structure

Understandable Level AA

Information and the operation of user interface must be understandable.

  • Clear and simple language
  • Predictable navigation and functionality
  • Input assistance and error identification
  • Consistent design patterns

Robust Level AA

Content must be robust enough to be interpreted by a wide variety of user agents and assistive technologies.

  • Valid, semantic HTML markup
  • Compatibility with screen readers
  • Support for assistive technologies
  • Future-proof accessibility features

3. Accessibility Features

Our platform includes numerous built-in accessibility features designed to support users with various needs:

Visual Accessibility

High Contrast Mode

Enhanced color contrast options for users with visual impairments or color vision deficiencies.

Text Scaling

Support for browser zoom up to 200% and customizable font sizes throughout the platform.

Alternative Text

Comprehensive alt text for images, graphics, and visual content to support screen readers.

Motor and Mobility Accessibility

Keyboard Navigation

Full keyboard accessibility with logical tab order and visible focus indicators.

Click Target Size

Appropriately sized clickable elements (minimum 44x44 pixels) for easy interaction.

Cognitive Accessibility

Clear Layout

Consistent, predictable layout with clear headings and logical content structure.

Error Prevention

Clear error messages, form validation, and confirmation dialogs for important actions.

Hearing Accessibility

Captions and Transcripts

Closed captions for videos and transcripts for audio content when available.

Visual Indicators

Visual alternatives to audio alerts and notifications throughout the platform.

4. Testing and Compliance

Regular Testing Methods

We employ multiple testing approaches to ensure accessibility compliance:

  • Automated Testing: Regular scans using tools like axe-core, WAVE, and Lighthouse
  • Manual Testing: Human evaluation of user interfaces and workflows
  • Screen Reader Testing: Testing with NVDA, JAWS, and VoiceOver
  • Keyboard Navigation Testing: Ensuring full functionality without a mouse
  • User Testing: Feedback from users with disabilities

Third-Party Audits

We regularly engage accessibility experts and organizations to conduct independent audits of our platform, including:

  • Annual comprehensive accessibility assessments
  • Specialized testing for new features and updates
  • Compliance verification against current standards
  • Recommendations for continuous improvement

Assistive Technology Compatibility

Our platform is tested and optimized for compatibility with:

  • Screen Readers: NVDA, JAWS, VoiceOver, TalkBack
  • Voice Recognition: Dragon NaturallySpeaking, Windows Speech Recognition
  • Switch Navigation: Various switch-activated devices
  • Magnification Software: ZoomText, MAGic, built-in OS magnifiers

  • Resolution Timeline: Provided based on issue complexity

Alternative Access

If you cannot access certain content or features due to accessibility barriers, we will work with you to provide alternative access methods, which may include:

  • Alternative formats for documents and media
  • Phone or email assistance for online processes
  • Temporary workarounds while we address the issue
  • Direct support from our accessibility team
